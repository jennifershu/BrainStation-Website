"Modern technology owes ecology an apology."

Electronic waste, or e-waste, describes electronic products that have become unwanted, useless, or obsolete, and have reached the end of their useful life. Most electronics contain materials such as beryllium, cadmium, mercury, and lead, which can all be harmful to the environment if improperly thrown away. This makes end-of-life management of e-waste extremely important.
